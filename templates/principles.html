Design Principles of Kolam

Unveiling the beauty and logic behind the patterns.

Symmetry

Kolam designs often exhibit radial or bilateral symmetry. The design is mirrored across one or more axes, creating a balanced and harmonious pattern. This symmetry is a fundamental aspect of Kolam art, reflecting the order and balance found in nature and the cosmos.

Repetition

Many Kolam designs incorporate repeated motifs or patterns. These repeating elements can be simple geometric shapes or more complex floral or animal forms. The repetition adds rhythm and visual interest to the design, creating a sense of continuity and flow.

Mathematical Logic

The creation of Kolam designs often involves mathematical principles, such as tessellations, geometric transformations, and modular arithmetic. These principles ensure the precision and regularity of the patterns, resulting in intricate and aesthetically pleasing designs. The underlying mathematical structure contributes to the overall beauty and complexity of the Kolam.
